What is Schizophrenia?

Schizophrenia is a chronic mental health condition that affects how a person thinks, feels, and behaves. It can cause hallucinations, delusions, disorganized speech, and impaired functioning. Common types include paranoid schizophrenia, borderline schizophrenia, and schizoaffective disorder.

Symptoms of Schizophrenia

  • Hallucinations (hearing voices or seeing things that aren't there)
  • Delusions (strongly held false beliefs)
  • Disorganized thoughts and speech
  • Withdrawal from family, friends, or society
  • Loss of motivation and emotional flatness

Why Early Detection Matters

Early signs of schizophrenia can appear in adolescence or early adulthood. A proper schizophrenia diagnosis test, followed by professional evaluation, improves the chances of managing symptoms effectively with therapy, medication, and support.
