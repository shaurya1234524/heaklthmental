Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) Test

Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) is a neurodevelopmental condition that affects how a person communicates, interacts, and experiences the world around them. The Autism-Spectrum Quotient (AQ) test is a screening tool that helps identify traits associated with ASD. It is designed to assess how often certain behaviors occur and how they impact daily functioning.

Why Take the ASD Test?

ASD is often identified early in childhood, but some individuals may not be diagnosed until later in life. This test helps provide an initial indication of whether someone might have characteristics of ASD. Taking this test is an important first step in recognizing potential autism traits. Please note that this test is not a definitive diagnosis but can help you decide whether you should seek further professional evaluation.

How It Works

The AQ test consists of 10 questions that assess various aspects of behavior and cognitive functioning. You will be asked to rate how often you experience certain behaviors on a scale of "Never" to "Very Often". Your score will provide insight into whether further evaluation for ASD may be necessary.

Test Results

Once you complete the test, you will receive a result based on your answers. If the score indicates a likelihood of ASD, you may be advised to seek further professional evaluation. If the score is low, it is unlikely that you have ASD, but this test should not replace a formal diagnosis from a healthcare professional.
