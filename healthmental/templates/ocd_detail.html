What is OCD?

OCD is a mental health disorder that affects people of all ages and walks of life. It occurs when a person gets caught in a cycle of obsessions and compulsions.

Common Symptoms

  • Excessive fear of germs or contamination
  • Needing things orderly and symmetrical
  • Aggressive thoughts towards self or others
  • Unwanted forbidden or taboo thoughts involving sex, religion, or harm
  • Compulsive cleaning or hand washing
  • Checking things repeatedly (locks, appliances, etc.)

Effects of OCD

OCD can severely impact daily functioning and quality of life. It can affect relationships, academic performance, and job productivity. Many people with OCD are aware that their thoughts and behaviors are irrational, yet they feel powerless to stop them.

Importance of Early Treatment

Early diagnosis and proper treatment can significantly improve outcomes. Treatment options include therapy (especially CBT), medication, and self-care practices. OCD is treatable, and many people learn to manage their symptoms effectively.

Y-BOCS (Yale-Brown Obsessive-Compulsive Scale)

The Y-BOCS is a gold-standard test used by mental health professionals to assess the severity of obsessive-compulsive symptoms. It evaluates the type, frequency, and intensity of both obsessions and compulsions.
