What is PHQ-9?

The PHQ-9 (Patient Health Questionnaire-9) is a widely used tool to assess the severity of depression in individuals. It is a self-report questionnaire consisting of 9 questions that help measure the presence of depressive symptoms.

Common Symptoms of Depression

  • Loss of interest in activities once enjoyed
  • Persistent feelings of sadness or hopelessness
  • Difficulty concentrating
  • Fatigue or lack of energy
  • Changes in appetite or weight
  • Sleeping too much or too little
  • Thoughts of death or suicide
  • Feeling guilty or worthless
  • Irritability or agitation

How the PHQ-9 Works

Each question in the PHQ-9 asks the individual to reflect on how often they have experienced a symptom in the past two weeks. They are then asked to rate the severity of the symptom from 0 (not at all) to 3 (nearly every day). The total score is used to determine the severity of depression:

  • 0-4: Minimal Depression
  • 5-9: Mild Depression
  • 10-14: Moderate Depression
  • 15-19: Moderately Severe Depression
  • 20-27: Severe Depression

Effects of Depression

Depression is a common but serious mental health condition that can negatively impact your well-being and quality of life. It can affect your ability to work, study, interact with others, and enjoy life. Depression can lead to complications like anxiety, substance abuse, and even physical health issues.

Importance of Treatment

While depression is a serious condition, it is treatable. A combination of therapy, medication, and self-care strategies can help manage and reduce symptoms. Early intervention can significantly improve recovery outcomes. If you think you may be experiencing symptoms of depression, it is important to seek professional help.
