What is the MDQ?

The Mood Disorder Questionnaire (MDQ) is a brief self-report screening tool designed to help detect symptoms of bipolar disorder. It consists of a checklist of mood-related symptoms and questions about the duration, severity, and impact of those symptoms on daily life.

Common Signs of Bipolar Disorder

  • Extreme mood swings (highs and lows)
  • Periods of unusually elevated or irritable mood
  • Inflated self-esteem or grandiosity
  • Decreased need for sleep
  • Racing thoughts or rapid speech
  • Engaging in risky behaviors
  • Depressive episodes with sadness, guilt, or fatigue

How the MDQ Works

The MDQ includes a series of yes/no questions about past and current experiences with elevated mood, increased activity, and related symptoms. It also asks about how these symptoms have co-occurred and how much they have impacted functioning. A positive screen indicates the need for further evaluation by a mental health professional.

Importance of Screening

Bipolar disorder can often go undiagnosed or be mistaken for depression. Early detection through tools like the MDQ helps ensure that individuals receive the proper care, therapy, and medication. Understanding mood patterns is the first step to managing symptoms effectively.
